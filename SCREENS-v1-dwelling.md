## Screen 7: Overhead and Profit Check

**Headline:**
> One important thing to check right now.

**Body:**
> Scroll toward the end of your estimate and look for a section that mentions
> **"Overhead"** and **"Profit"** — sometimes shown as **"O&P."**
>
> These are legitimate costs that any reputable contractor charges to manage
> a rebuild project. They should appear as a line item — typically around
> 10% each (20% combined) of the total job cost.

**Question:**
> Can you find "Overhead" and "Profit" (or "O&P") listed somewhere in
> your estimate?

Choices:
- Yes, I can see it listed with a dollar amount
- I see something that might be it, but I'm not sure
- I cannot find it anywhere in the estimate
- I'm not sure what I'm looking at

**Helper text:**
> If Overhead and Profit are missing or very low (under 10% combined),
> this is a common issue that can significantly reduce your settlement.
> We'll add this to your summary.

**Editable text area — pre-populated example output:**

ESTIMATE REVIEW NOTES
Prepared: [date]
Property: [user's estimate nickname]

COVERAGE SECTIONS

The "Building Code Upgrades" section does not appear in the estimate or
shows $0. This section is legally required in most jurisdictions when a
home is rebuilt and can represent 10% or more of total rebuild cost.
Please confirm this has been included and itemized.

Overhead and Profit (O&P) does not appear to be clearly listed in the
estimate. Standard contractor O&P for a whole-home rebuild is typically
around 10% each for Overhead and Profit. Please confirm these are
included.

SYSTEMS

No section for HVAC was found in the estimate. Please confirm heating,
ventilation, and air conditioning replacement has been included.

No section for fire sprinklers was found. If the home had a sprinkler
system, please confirm it is included.

LIVING ROOM

The living room had upgraded stone tile flooring and a custom coffered
ceiling. Please confirm these finishes are reflected in the estimate,
not generic or standard-grade equivalents.

A built-in entertainment center was present. Please confirm this is
listed as a separate line item.

KITCHEN

Custom cabinetry and stone countertops were present. Please confirm
these are listed at appropriate grade, not standard or builder-grade.

SITE AND ACCESS

The property is located on a steep hillside with a narrow access road.
Please confirm that access difficulty and any required special equipment
have been factored into labor costs.

There is a drainage or soil issue on the lot. Please confirm this has
been scoped and estimated.

OTHER STRUCTURES

A detached garage and pool were present on the property. Please confirm
both are addressed in the estimate.

This review was prepared using the Estimate Review Helper, informed by
guidance from United Policyholders (uphelp.org). This is not legal advice.
For complex claims, consider consulting a licensed public adjuster or
attorney.

## Global UI Notes for Engineer

- **Progress indicator:** persistent across all checklist screens.
  Show current section and total remaining (e.g., "Systems · Step 2 of 5").
- **Back button:** always visible; never loses prior answers.
- **"I'm not sure" option:** always available; contributes a soft flag
  to the summary ("You were unsure whether X was included — worth
  confirming with your adjuster").
- **NOTE callouts:** wherever a question touches something likely to be
  in a NOTE in the estimate (e.g., "check for a note about site
  conditions near the foundation section"), add a small tip: 
  💡 "Look for notes in that section — good estimates explain their
  assumptions."
- **Empty state summary:** if user answers "No" or "I'm not sure" to
  everything: "Based on your answers, no major gaps stood out. If
  something still feels off, trust that instinct — come back and add
  notes at any time."
- **Auto-save indicator:** small unobtrusive text: "Progress saved on
  this device ✓"
- **PDF viewer:** show PDF alongside checklist on desktop (side-by-side);
  on mobile, offer a "View my estimate" toggle that opens PDF in a
  bottom sheet, then returns to questions.
